GRE scores range from 260 to 340. A score above 310 is considered competitive for top universities.

TOEFL scores range from 0 to 120. Most universities require at least 90.

CGPA above 8.0 significantly increases chances of admission.

Work experience improves chances for MBA and MS programs.

Public universities generally have lower tuition fees compared to private universities.

Scholarships are awarded based on academic merit, financial need, or research potential.

Computer Science programs usually require strong mathematics background.

Statement of Purpose (SOP) plays a major role in admission decisions.

Letters of Recommendation (LOR) should ideally come from professors or employers.

Universities consider GRE, TOEFL, CGPA, SOP, LOR, and research experience during evaluation.
